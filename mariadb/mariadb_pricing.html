Technical Questions about Cloud Hosting for MariaDB™

What MariaDB™ version do you support?

Currently, we support MariaDB™ 10.5 only.

Does your managed hosting for MariaDB™ support SSL connection?

Yes. The SSL connection is enabled by default for each newly created MariaDB™ on our cloud hosting platform for MariaDB™.

Do you do backups for my online MariaDB™ database?

Yes. Our Kubernetes platform takes backups regularly. The backup frequency varies by hosting plan. Please note that we implement weekly backup for free-trial applications.

Can I take backups of my MariaDB™ in your managed hosting for MariaDB™?

Yes. You can back up your MariaDB™ via our hosting control panel and download them.

Do you have access limits to my MariaDB™ in the managed hosting service for MariaDB™?

No. We do not set limits on your access to MariaDB™.

How can I manage my MariaDB™ databases and users?

You can manage users in the control panel in our cloud hosting for MariaDB™. For detailed steps, please refer to the documentation.

Is your cloud hosting for MariaDB™ shared?

No. Our fully managed hosting for MariaDB™ on Kubernetes is based on standalone MariaDB™ instance.

What cloud providers and regions do you support in your cloud hosting for MariaDB™?

We currently support the private cloud from our four data centers in Dallas, Bend, Charlotte, and Denver. We are building more data centers and will support most of the popular public cloud providers in more regions in the near future.

What MariaDB™ storage engine do you support?

We currently support InnoDB in our managed hosting for MariaDB™.

How can I migrate my MariaDB™ database onto your platform?

We support the standard MariaDB™ tool to restore your local backup onto our Kubernetes-based platform.

Can I monitor the resource usage on managed hosting plans for MariaDB™?

Yes. Our control panel supports real-time resource usage monitoring. Please refer to this guide article on how you can do it.

Do you support MariaDB™ Cluster?

No. We support single-instance MariaDB™ deployment.

What is the default maximum number of connections for online MariaDB™?

The maximum connections are 1000.

Do you provide root user for online MariaDB™?

No, we do not provide a root user by default. However, clients can create super users in our client panel.
